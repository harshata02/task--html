What is poverty?

world bank defines it as condition in which people surviving on less than $1.90 a day. It is vicious cycle having multi dimensional issues like lack of education,basic sanitation,lower standard of living and poor health facilties.

Ranking of poverty in india.

As per UNDP data nearly 15% of indians are multidimesionally poor,and 23% are living below purchasing power parity.

Reasons for poverty

1.Structural nature of employement due to inadequate productive capacity. 2.Lack of skills and low pace of research and development. 3.Low saving rate. 4.Corruption and diversion of resources. 5.Mismatch of demand supply gaps. 6.static nature of agriculture growth and its low gdp contribution. 7.increasing urbanisation and uncontrolled popultion growth.

steps to counter poverty.

1.Identification of actual benefeciaries through proper utilisation of database. 2.utilisation of IT for better targeting and building information syymetry. 3.Giving push to MSMEs and entereprenuerships. 4.Increasing financial inclusion programmes. 5.taxing the pro-rich and counter diversion of resources.
